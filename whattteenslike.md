# Pop-Culture Reference Guide for MGIN: What 17–19-Year-Old Austrian HTL Students Actually Watch, Play, and Meme in 2026

## TL;DR
- For this cohort (born ~2007–2009), the digital "big three" dominate everything else: **music (93% regular use), short-video/social (Snapchat, TikTok, Instagram, all near 64–65% in Austria), and gaming (70%)** — with the smartphone (231 min/day per JIM-Studie 2025) as the single hub. Build lessons around these and you reach almost everyone.
- The strongest *shared* 2026 touchpoints for cold opens: **Italian Brainrot memes (Tralalero Tralala, Tung Tung Tung Sahur, Ballerina Cappuccina), Roblox/Minecraft/Fortnite, ChatGPT, KPop Demon Hunters, Wednesday S2 & Stranger Things 5, and German-language rap/R&B (Pashanim, Jazeek, Zartmann)**.
- The most reliable *nostalgia* lever (childhood ~2010–2018): **Pokémon, Beyblade, Monster High, the Nintendo DS/Wii, Hannah Montana/iCarly/Victorious, H2O – Plötzlich Meerjungfrau, Subway Surfers/Temple Run/Flappy Bird, Gangnam Style, and early German YouTubers (Gronkh, Y-Titty, DieLochis, BibisBeautyPalace)**.

## Key Findings

**1. Sourcing & method.** The strongest data for this exact group comes from two annual studies: the **Jugend-Internet-Monitor 2026** (Saferinternet.at, Austrian 11–17-year-olds) for social/apps, and the **JIM-Studie 2025** (mpfs, German 12–19-year-olds, n=1,200, fielded June–July 2025, published November 2025 — the gold standard) for relative media importance, games, creators and time-use. Austrian 17–19 data specifically is thin, so for most categories I generalize to Austrian/German teens; international Gen Z data is used for memes, music, film and global apps. Each section flags its geography.

**2. Relative importance is clear and quantifiable.** Per JIM 2025, the most-used media are *"fürs Internet (96 %) und zum Musikhören (93 %)"*, and the most-used device remains the smartphone (98%). Regular (daily/several-times-weekly) use ranks: **Smartphone 98% · Internet 96% · Music 93% · Online video/YouTube 83% · Digital games 70% · Streaming (Netflix etc.) 70% · Radio 51% · Linear TV 50% · Books 33% · Podcasts ~25% · Magazines/e-books ~10%.** Daily time: smartphone 231 min (rising to over 4½ hours for 18–19-year-olds), music streaming 136 min, video streaming 109 min, gaming 88 min, YouTube 80 min, books 49 min. **Takeaway for MGIN: music, social video and gaming are the universal languages; print magazines are nearly dead as a reference point; AI is now native (see below) — a gift for a bioinformatics teacher.**

**3. AI is the fastest-rising item — and on-theme.** Per JIM 2025, *"ChatGPT ist die mit Abstand wichtigste Anwendung unter den Zwölf- bis 19-Jährigen: 84 Prozent haben das Tool bereits verwendet – im Jahr 2024 waren es noch 57 Prozent"* (+27 percentage points). 91% use at least one AI tool (up from 62% in 2024), and ChatGPT is now the #2 research tool behind search engines.

**4. Gender split matters for examples.** Gaming and gaming-adjacent platforms skew strongly male: JIM 2025 gives **Discord (boys 32%, girls 7%) and Twitch (boys 18%, girls 4%)**, while girls lead on **Pinterest (girls 36%, boys 5%)**; gaming is done regularly by 83% of boys vs 58% of girls (boys 114 min/day vs girls 59). Snapchat, Instagram, BookTok/romantasy reading and beauty/lifestyle creators skew female. Memes, Minecraft, Roblox, music and streaming are the most gender-neutral — safest for whole-class hooks.

## Relative Importance of the Categories (the section to teach from)
Ordering the categories by how much they matter to this cohort's daily life and identity (synthesizing JIM 2025 time-use + Austrian Jugend-Internet-Monitor + Gen-Z research):

1. **Social/messaging + short video (the social spine).** WhatsApp for logistics; Snapchat/TikTok/Instagram for identity, status and belonging. Near-universal, highly daily, strongest FOMO driver. *Highest classroom ROI.*
2. **Music.** 93% regular use, 136 min/day streaming — the most constant companion and a core identity marker. Gender-neutral.
3. **Gaming.** 70% regular but sharply gendered (boys 114 min/day vs girls 59). Minecraft/Roblox/Fortnite are the most universal; esports and Discord are deep but male-skewed.
4. **Streaming series/film.** 70% regular; communal "everyone watched Wednesday/Stranger Things" moments; binge culture.
5. **YouTube/creators/influencers.** 83% watch online video regularly; creators are genuine role models and shape language, humor and purchasing.
6. **Memes/internet culture.** Not a "time budget" line but the connective tissue of humor and in-group identity — and in 2025–26 (Italian Brainrot) inseparable from AI.
7. **AI tools (ChatGPT).** Newly central (84% have used it) — both a study tool and a culture topic; uniquely relevant to bioinformatics.
8. **Books.** A minority but passionate (BookTok/romantasy) subculture, strongly female.
9. **Linear TV / game shows / reality TV.** Declining but still shared family/second-screen viewing; reality and game shows offer ready algorithm hooks.
10. **Print magazines.** Near-irrelevant (~10%); useful only as nostalgia (BRAVO) or for a specific stats example (Panini).

## Recommendations
**Stage 1 — Safest universal hooks (use first, reach everyone):**
- **Italian Brainrot as a recurring mascot/narrative.** Use Tralalero Tralala or Ballerina Cappuccina as a "lab assistant" character across lessons — and use it honestly to teach what generative AI does (image+voice synthesis), which is on-theme for MGIN.
- **Minecraft/Roblox for algorithms & randomness** (world seeds, procedural generation, drop rates).
- **Music + Spotify Wrapped for biostatistics** — "your Wrapped is descriptive statistics": top artists = mode, minutes listened = totals/means, genre breakdown = categorical distributions.

**Stage 2 — Themed examples by topic:**
- **Probability/combinatorics:** "Are You The One?" matching algorithm; Wer-wird-Millionär joker logic; Panini sticker-album coupon-collector problem.
- **Distributions/sampling:** Flappy Bird/Temple Run scores; Panem "reaping" lottery.
- **Exponential/logistic growth:** Gangnam Style's billion-view milestone; influencer follower curves; MrBeast view counts.
- **AI/ML for bioinformatics:** ChatGPT and Photomath as a "tool vs. understanding" discussion; Cassandra/KPop Demon Hunters as cold opens on AI in culture.

**Stage 3 — Nostalgia "cold opens" to build rapport:** open occasional lessons with a 2010–2018 throwback (Pokémon, Beyblade, Hannah Montana, the Wii, Subway Surfers, Gangnam Style) before pivoting to the day's bioinformatics content. This signals "I know your world" and lowers the affective filter.

**Benchmarks that should change your choices:**
- If the class skews heavily male (typical for HTL technical streams), lean into **gaming/esports/Discord** examples; if more mixed, prioritize **music, memes, streaming** to avoid alienating the minority.
- If you notice references landing flat, **re-poll the class each semester** — meme cycles (esp. brainrot) turn over in months; social-platform usage is *declining* in 2026, so verify before relying on any single app.
- Keep **AI/ChatGPT** central regardless — it is both the fastest-growing item and the one most aligned with bioinformatics.

## Caveats
- **Geography:** Hard Austrian 17–19 data is scarce. Social/app figures are Austrian (Saferinternet.at, 11–17); media-importance, games, creators and time-use are German (JIM 2025, 12–19); music is Austria+Germany Spotify; memes/film/global apps are international. The 17–19 segment games and reads somewhat less, and uses AI and Instagram somewhat more, than the 11–15 segment within these studies.
